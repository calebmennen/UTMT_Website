  • What is Mock Trial?
    Collegiate mock trial is an extracurricular activity in which schools
    from across the nation compete against one another in simulated court proceedings.
    In a round, one team plays the part of "Prosecution/Plantiff" while the other team
    plays the part of "Defense". Scores are given by judges based on the quality of
    performance given by each team. The team with the most points at the end of the
    round wins that judge's ballot.

Who Are We?

We represent the University of Tennessee in Mock Trial competitions across the country.

Mock Trial

The American Mock Trial Association (AMTA) "was founded in 1985 by Dean Richard Calkins of Drake Law School. AMTA serves as the governing body for intercollegiate mock trial competition.

Our Purpose

The purpose of this organization is to educate undergraduate students at the University of Tennessee in the areas of public law, speaking skills, critical thinking, and argument ability, and to have fun doing it.

Our Team

Our team is made up of many talented students both with and without legal aspirations. We only take the best of the best of the best, and we're one of the
best teams in our region.
